Personal Background – For Clinical Assessment

I am a 37-year-old male with Brazilian, UK, and Portuguese nationality, currently undergoing a mental health assessment through the NHS. I have been seen by a GP, a psychologist, and a psychiatrist at Ealing MINT Mental Health Services. I have a second face-to-face appointment with a psychiatrist scheduled in approximately 10 days.

I am writing this to provide background information that I find difficult to communicate verbally. This text reflects my own experiences and perceptions; I understand that any diagnosis or clinical conclusion will be made by the assessing professionals.

During childhood, I walked on my toes and experienced significant distress. I was bullied, called gay, told I had demons, and I experienced panic attacks with sweating, dizziness, and fear in places such as school, shopping centres, buses, and other public environments. I was referred to a psychologist at school, and at age 18, a psychiatrist in Brazil prescribed clonazepam and fluoxetine. I exhibited behaviours such as hand movements and rocking.

As an adult, I experience significant difficulties with communication and social interaction. I struggle with noise, smells, touch, and sensory overload. I often do not understand irony or jokes, which can lead to irritation and unintentional rudeness. This has caused issues at work, including incidents as recent as today.

I have very fixed routines. I wear the same types of clothes and eat the same foods repeatedly. I collect Rubik’s cubes and can focus for many hours, often overnight, on coding, which is currently my main interest.

I started taking mirtazapine when I weighed 62kg and now weigh 74kg, which has been a positive change. My manager, colleagues, and family have all expressed concern about my mental health, as have I. These difficulties seem to come and go. I experienced similar issues around six years ago and attended a GP in the UK with complaints of chest and abdominal pain.

My social life outside of work is extremely limited. I have no partner, no social visits, and only two close friends (one current coworker and one former coworker). I have previously expressed a desire to get married, and several past partners asked me to marry them. I ultimately left the country in those relationships. I believe my difficulties and low self-esteem contributed to these outcomes, and I am reluctant to involve someone else while I am struggling.

I experience physical symptoms such as eye pain, nausea, and a feeling of wanting to vomit. My eyes sometimes move involuntarily, and colleagues have laughed at this. I overthink extensively, cry easily (both in positive and negative situations), and feel a strong need to ensure I am understood. I am very picky with food.

At times, especially when overwhelmed, I feel a desire to disappear. As a child, I caused my mother significant distress. I remember a moment when she hugged me crying, asking what was wrong, as I had become afraid to leave the house due to panic attacks. I lost a school year. Over time, I have lost jobs, relationships, and friendships.

I struggle significantly with change. If I have a plan, deviating from it requires a large amount of energy. I require recovery time in every job I have held. I have previously been dismissed from jobs due to workplace changes that I could not adapt to.

My sleep is poor. I wake several times during the night. Medication helps, but I often wake feeling sedated, similar to a hangover, which makes working difficult. I experience anxiety, dissociation (things sometimes do not feel real), and my mind attempts to make sense of random noises, which can feel like internal voices that are self-policing rather than external. When this happens, I sometimes press my toes into the floor to ground myself.

During the pandemic, I spent extended periods sitting on the floor and sometimes sleeping on the floor.

This text is not exhaustive. I likely missed important details, as this was written in one sitting to ensure something was documented prior to my next consultation.
